FAQ

Answers to Every Question Indian Students Ask

Can't find what you're looking for? Chat with our AI assistant or book a free call — we'll answer everything in detail, honestly.

Is MBBS from Europe valid to practise in India?
+
Yes. All universities we partner with are NMC (formerly MCI) recognised. After graduating, you take the FMGE exam to obtain your Indian medical licence. We help you prepare for FMGE from Day 1 of your studies abroad.
What NEET score do I need for MBBS abroad?
+
Most European countries require you to clear the NEET cutoff (50th percentile) but do not require a very high rank for admission. Serbia, Romania, and Hungary typically work with scores from 400 upwards. We assess your profile and suggest the most realistic options honestly.
What is Ausbildung and how is it different from a degree?
+
Ausbildung is Germany's dual vocational training system — you work 3–4 days a week at a company (earning €700–€1,200/month) while attending vocational school for the remaining days. It takes 2–3 years. It's ideal if you want to enter the German workforce quickly, earn while you learn, and build a path to permanent residency. Whether it's better than a degree depends entirely on your career goals — we help you decide.
How long does the visa process take?
+
For Germany (National D Visa), processing typically takes 8–12 weeks from submission. For Schengen countries like Poland and Hungary, student visa processing takes 4–10 weeks. We begin visa preparation 4–5 months before your departure to ensure there's no last-minute risk.
Do you charge the student or the university?
+
We charge only the student — never a university commission. This means our recommendations are entirely based on what's best for your profile, not what earns us more. Our fee structure is transparent and disclosed before you sign anything.
Can my parents visit me while I study abroad?
+
Yes. Indian parents can apply for a Schengen or national visit visa for most European countries. We advise families on the correct visa category, required documentation, and the best timing to apply. Many of our students have had their families visit within the first year of studies.
